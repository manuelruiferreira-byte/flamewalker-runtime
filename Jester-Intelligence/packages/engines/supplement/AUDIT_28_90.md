# ACE Mind Optimizer Commit 4 Audit

This audit validates the canonical registry, four orthogonal layer engines, and the individual stack assembler before live application integration.

## Fields

### Clean 28-day field

- Four complete Monday-to-Sunday windows
- Green body-state inputs
- Seven rotating operational and symbolic archetypes
- Selected members written to external history after each day
- Deterministic replay for identical output and hash

### Adversarial 90-day field

- Thirteen weekly windows
- Periodic nervous-system and sleep holds
- Periodic liver and heart conditional states
- Changing domain and symbolic fields
- Sequential persistence history
- Every selected stack checked for holds, conflicts, burden ceilings, class caps, slot caps, and deterministic replay

### Physical-demand conflict field

- Three strong physical-demand days in one week
- Cordyceps selected on the first two permitted days
- The third physical-demand day collides with constitutional NMN-H
- Cordyceps remains visible with an explicit pairing-conflict reason

## Capacity invariant

The registry contains 87 nominal weekly item-use targets. Time caps permit at most 77 member slots per week:

- Morning: 4 per day
- Afternoon: 4 per day
- Night: 3 per day

All nominal targets therefore cannot be equally mandatory. Frequency priorities are divided into constitutional, governed, conditional, and maintenance tiers.

## Coverage

The combined suite contains 24 layer checks, 40 focused optimizer checks, and 96 sequential audit checks. It covers mandatory companions, NAD mutual exclusion and rotation, conditional Cordyceps visibility, weekly governance, residual timing, exclusions, conflicts, body permission, benefit overlap, burden ceilings, time caps, label orthogonality, causal explanations, serialization, deterministic hashing, and critical-data holds.

The body vectors and symbolic correspondences are scheduling heuristics. This audit proves internal consistency and deterministic execution, not clinical efficacy.
